From: Marcus Mason
Congratulations — you're under contract on 1840 N. Bissell. This is the part of the process where the rhythm changes, and I want to set expectations now so the next 5–6 weeks feel less mysterious. The next 30–45 days have a real cadence: Week 1 — Attorney review & inspection. David takes the lead on contract review. We get the inspection done (Wednesday). Most active week. Lots of decisions. Week 2 — Inspection negotiation & loan paperwork. David and the seller's attorney work out inspection asks. You're submitting documents to Janelle. Weeks 3–4 — Quiet stretch. This is the part that surprises people. Appraisal happens. Underwriting happens. From your end, nothing is visible. This silence is normal. If something is actually wrong, I'll be the one to tell you. Don't read silence as a problem. Week 5 — Clear to close, final walkthrough, closing. Things speed up again. How I'll keep you in the loop: You'll get a status update from me every Friday, even when nothing is happening. If something changes mid-week, you hear about it within hours, not days. If you ever feel like you don't know what's going on, text me — that's a signal I missed something, and I'll fix it. A few things not to worry about: • David being quiet for stretches — he's working when there's something to act on. • The appraisal being slightly under list — common, usually negotiable, not a deal-killer. • Last-minute "conditions" from underwriting in week 4 — almost every loan gets these. Talk Wednesday post-inspection. — Marcus
