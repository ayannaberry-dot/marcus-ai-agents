Decision-log entry

Date: 2026-04-28

Decision: Drop list price on 4502 N. Hermitage from $819,000 to $799,000 (−2.4%).

Context: Two weeks active on MLS. 14 showings, no offers. Buyer-agent feedback consistent: kitchen and block strong; split primary-bath layout is a meaningful drag on a subset of buyers.

Why:
– 14 showings with no offer signals a price-to-perceived-value gap. Either the price is wrong or the property has a feature drag we underestimated.
– The split-bath issue showed up in CMA notes and showing-host rollup. It's not a defect that fixes itself by waiting.
– Sitting at 30+ DOM has a known cost: buyer-agent perception turns from "fresh" to "what's wrong with it." Better to recalibrate at 14 than 30.
– $799K still implies $367/sqft, defensible against the comp set; we're not giving away the block premium.

Alternative considered: Hold price 1 more week, run an open house at current price, decide after. Rejected because the showing data is already saturated; another 7 days of showings won't change the conclusion, just the timeline.

Stakeholders informed: Trans (call scheduled today 4:30 PM), Javier (executes price change in MLS), photographer Anya (no change needed).

Reversal criteria: If 5+ showings in week 3 produce no offer, consider $785K. If an offer comes in at $799K within 7 days, validates the call.
