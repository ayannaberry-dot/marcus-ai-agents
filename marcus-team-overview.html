Built for Marcus · v0.1

Your team. Your way.

You and Javier already do the work of a 5-person brokerage. This is the system that makes it feel that way to your clients — without you typing more emails or missing a call back. Everything here either takes work off your plate, or makes sure no one feels ghosted while you're in another showing.

How to think about this in 60 seconds

Read this part once. The rest is reference.

It's a team of helpers, not a piece of software.

Each helper has one job — like a small staff. You don't have to "open" anything. Most of the time, you talk into your pocketAI, or you say one phrase to your phone, and the right helper picks it up. Javier reviews and sends.

1. You talk. Phone calls + in-person convos (with consent) get recorded by your pocketAI. That's the raw material.
2. Helpers sort it. The right helper turns the conversation into a draft, a follow-up, a TODO, or a client note.
3. A human sends it. Operational stuff → Javier hits send. Relational stuff → you hit send. Nothing goes out without one of you reviewing.

Start here. Just these three things in week 1.

If you only do these three, the system pays for itself. Everything else can wait until you bump into a need for it.

  1. Wear your pocketAI every day. Don't think about it. The whole system runs on what it captures. Just remember: in-person conversations need a quick "hey, mind if I record this for my notes?" — that's Illinois law and the consent script lives in your team's pocketAI helper.
  2. Use the morning Daily Debrief. While you're brushing your teeth, say "start my debrief." It will walk you through your active clients, what's slipping, and what one thing matters most today. This is the "AI that calls me" idea you wanted.
  3. After every showing, say "log a quick debrief." 60 seconds. Address, client, gut take, one thing good, one thing bad, next step. The system files it under the right client and queues your follow-up draft before you even leave the parking spot.

Common questions

The "wait, but…" questions that come up when people first see this.

Do I have to learn 31 different things?

No. You'll really only talk to four or five of them — Daily Debrief, Showing Debrief, Log a Decision, Voice Memo Dump, and the pocketAI search ("what did I tell so-and-so about…"). Everything else either runs in the background or gets used by Javier. The full list is here so you and Javier know what's available when a need shows up.

What if I don't trust the AI to send something?

Then you don't. Nothing here sends without a human reviewing it. Operational stuff (showing requests, FAQ replies, weekly recaps) Javier reviews and sends. Anything relational — showing follow-ups, lost-bid messages, anything where the relationship is the message — only goes out when you hit send. The team's job is to get the draft to "almost done" so you're not staring at a blank screen.

What happens if Javier is sick?

The drafts still get made. They just sit in a queue waiting for someone to send them. You can hit send yourself in a pinch — every helper writes drafts that read like Javier wrote them, with your sign-off. The bigger insurance is the SOP library: if you ever need to onboard a backup VA, the system Javier uses is documented, not in his head.

What about the recording rules?

Phone calls in Illinois only need one party to consent — you, on your own calls. In-person conversations are two-party: you need to ask. The pocketAI helper has a 12-second consent script you can read, and it auto-pauses recording until consent is given. Personal calls (family, doctor, etc.) auto-quarantine and never enter the corpus.

What if a helper is doing something dumb?

Tell Claude — in plain English. You don't need to know code or markdown.

Small fixes: Open Claude with your team folder selected and say something like: "The tour-builder helper keeps forgetting parking notes. Open the SKILL.md at 01_buyer_side/skills/tour-builder/ and update it so parking is always checked first." Claude rewrites the file for you. Test it on a real task. If it's still wrong, tell Claude what's still off — it'll iterate.

Bigger fixes: If a helper has a pattern problem — wrong tone, missing context, routing to the wrong folder — say: "Review the [helper name] skill end-to-end. It's been doing [X]. Tell me what's wrong and propose a fix." Claude reads the SKILL.md, walks you through what to change, and only edits when you say go.

Retire a helper: If after 30 days a helper isn't earning its place, say: "Archive the [helper name] skill — we're not using it." Claude moves it out of the active folder so it stops loading, but keeps the file around in case you change your mind.

You own this system. Nothing here requires anyone else to maintain it.

Setting up your team — one-time, ~2 hours

Self-service from end to end. You'll need: a laptop, your phone, your pocketAI device, and the cloud link to your team folder.

First time setting this up? Read these 7 steps top to bottom. Already running? You probably don't need this section — refer to it when adding a new tool or onboarding Javier.
1

Get your team folder onto your computer10 min

Your team folder ("Marcus Real Estate Team") was delivered to you as a cloud link — Dropbox, Google Drive, or similar. Open the link and download the whole folder.

Save it here, exactly: ~/Documents/Claude/Projects/Marcus Real Estate Team/

Don't move it after that — Claude looks for it in this location. If you want it backed up to the cloud, drag the folder into a synced Dropbox or Google Drive folder first, and then point Claude at the synced location. (This matters in step 4 — Javier needs the same folder.)

2

Install Claude on your computer15 min

  • Go to claude.ai/download and install the desktop app for Mac or Windows.
  • Sign in with your email. If you don't have an account, create one — use your work email so receipts and access are clean.
  • Pick a paid plan. Pro is the minimum; Max gives you more capacity if you'll be running the system heavily (which you will be once Javier's also using it).
  • Open Claude. Go to Settings → Desktop app → Cowork mode and turn it on. Cowork is what lets Claude read and write files in your team folder.

Point Claude at your team folder2 min

  • In the Claude app, click "Select folder" (or "Connect a workspace").
  • Navigate to ~/Documents/Claude/Projects/Marcus Real Estate Team/ and select it.
  • That's it. Claude now has access to all 31 helpers + the data folder + the SOP library.

Quick test: in the Claude chat, type "list the helpers in my team". You should see the directorate folders. If you see them, you're good.
