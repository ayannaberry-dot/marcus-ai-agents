Hi all — Last Saturday I was in a kitchen in Lincoln Park with a buyer who'd fallen in love with the house. The light, the layout, the block — all of it. Then we went to the basement and I told her to walk away. It wasn't the basement that was the problem. It was that the smell of damp told me a story the listing didn't, and the price didn't reflect what fixing that story would cost. She didn't want to hear it. I would've made the commission either way. But buying a house with a hidden water history at the top of a market like this one, in your first home, isn't the kind of decision I want a client of mine to make on a Saturday morning. She walked. Three days later we had a different property under contract — better block, better bones, no hidden story. That's the part of this job that doesn't show up on Zillow. The May market read: • Median price in Chicago city: $355K, up 2.1% YoY • Days on market: 23 (down from 31 last May) • Inventory still tight everywhere under $500K — biggest mismatch in West Loop and Lakeview • Detached single-family in Ravenswood / North Center: still moving in 2 weeks at list Three blocks I've been watching this month: • Wrightwood west of Sheffield (Lincoln Park): the quieter half. Three solid listings cleared in 30 days. Underrated. • Hermitage near Lawrence (Ravenswood): reno inventory finally hit, and pricing is honest. Best entry into Ravenswood right now. • Adams between Ashland and Loomis (West Loop): if you've been priced out of West Loop south, this stretch is sneaking under $475K for 1-bds. If you're thinking about a move this summer or know someone who is, reply to this and we'll talk. The market is moving differently than it looks from the outside. — Marcus
